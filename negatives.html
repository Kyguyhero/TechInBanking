Negatives

Cybersecurity

Despite the numerous regulations put in place to safeguard customers, like Regulation E which protects users when they utilize elctronic funds, there are still significant risks that customers face while online.

The increased reliance on computers and digital systems in banking has exposed financial institutions to a higher risk of cybersecurity threats. Hackers and cybercriminals continuously target banks to gain unauthorized access to sensitive customer data, commit fraud, or disrupt operations.

Furthermore, with the digitization of banking, the potential for data breaches and privacy breaches has increased. If banks fail to implement robust security measures, they may be vulnerable to data breaches that expose customer information, including personal and financial details.

Internet Reliance

Banking continues to grow increasingly reliant on technology, which means that technology has to work consistently or customers face issues that can lead to backlash and loss of business.

In the event of system outages or prolonged downtime, customers may experience disruptions in accessing their accounts, making transactions, or accessing customer support. These interruptions can lead to frustration, inconvenience, and potential financial losses for both banks and customers.

While computers and digital banking have made financial services more accessible to many, there are still segments of the population, particularly older individuals, who may face challenges in adapting to digital banking platforms. This digital divide can lead to financial exclusion and hinder access to banking services for certain demographics.

In addition, technology has reduced the need for face-to-face interactions with bank tellers and other employees. This makes it difficult to address complex financial needs and other issues.
